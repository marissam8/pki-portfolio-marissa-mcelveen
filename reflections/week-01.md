## Prompts

1. This week I learned about digital certs, their components and their significance within PKI. I also learned why private keys are important.
2. The most challenging concept for me this week was the trust store. 
3. This concept is present everywhere in the real-world; on operating systems, devices, and within applications that contains a collection of pre-approved digital certificates, likely Root Certificate Authority (CA) certificates.
4. I would a explain trust store to a non-technical person by imagining you going on vacation and you need to book a stay. You decide to use air bnb. You are using a site which list of  strangers' homes as the place to stay. You see a listing for a house that looks perfect, but how do you know it’s safe to stay there and that the host is legitimate? Airbnb itself acts like a trust anchor. Airbnb verifies the host’s identity, ensures the listing is real, and guarantees certain standards. Because you trust Airbnb, you automatically trust that any listing verified by Airbnb is safe.

5. My reamining question is, are trust stores and trust anchors used interchangeably? If not what is the difference?
